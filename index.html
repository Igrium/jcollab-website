ABOUT

The Perpetual Testing Initiative is a masterclass in comedy writing - an amalgamation of random bullshit that forms a coherent, though somewhat absurd, story.

Yet, the individual worlds in this vast multiverse feel strikingly unremarkable. A voice line cracks open a window into wherever we've stumbled into, yet the test chamber itself seems entirely unrelated.

That's where you come in. Choose a voice line and build a map around it. What does this universe look like? How is it unique? Time to ﬂex your Hammer skills!

At the end, each submission will be assembled into a mod, telling the "quest for the money-verse" in its full, chaotic glory!

INSTRUCTIONS

To begin, go to the supplied spreadsheet and sign up.

Lines are divided into three categories:

  • One-off: Most Cave variants only appears in one chamber. These are good for solo mappers looking for inspiration. It's up to you to interpret how this world appears!

  • Series: Some universes span multiple levels, forming a "series". Each level in a given series must have continuity, making it ideal for small teams desiring something a little more ambitious. Is this a mini-campaign with its own story, or just a series of consecutive chambers?

  • Prime: Voice lines belonging to Cave Prime don't specify anything about the universe you're in, leaving room for endless possibilities! Just make sure the art-style stays coherent.

Once the deadline arrives, each submitted map is judged against others using the same voice line (series are judged as a whole); the winner for any given universe gets placed in the mod campaign!

Runner-ups are still included in the mod as extras.

RULES

  1. Include your assigned voice line. It should ideally play at the beginning of the map as the player is leaving the elevator (if applicable). Do not play any other voice lines.

  2. Maps must be original. You may remaster puzzlemaker maps with the author's permission, but you need to add enough additional substance (visuals, bts gameplay, etc) to consider it transformative.

  3. Hammer maps only. This project is about breaking away from the sterile, puzzlemaker aesthetic! As stated above, you are allowed to design the puzzle in the puzzlemaker first, but it must be finalized in Hammer.

  4. Must be singleplayer. The mod will be singleplayer, so maps must be singleplayer (duh). Ideally, you should play as a "generic test subject" with no specific backstory.

  5. No copyrighted assets. You must have permission to use (and redistribute) any third-party assets. Be sure to declare any required attribution when submitting the map. Valve assets are excepted.

  6. Don't publicly release prior to event completion. You may post it on the workshop after the mod is released, but don't ruin it by preemptively spoiling your level!

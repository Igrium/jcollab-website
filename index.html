ABOUT

The Perpetual Testing Initiative is a masterclass in comedy writing — an amalgamation of random bullshit that somehow forms a coherent, if completely absurd, story.

Yet, the individual worlds in this vast multiverse feel strikingly unremarkable. A voice line cracks open a window into wherever we've stumbled into, but the test chamber itself seems entirely unrelated.

That's where you come in. Choose a voice line and build a map around it. What does this universe look like? How is it unique? Time to ﬂex your Hammer skills!

At the end, each submission will be assembled into a mod, telling the "quest for the money-verse" in its full, chaotic glory!

INSTRUCTIONS

To begin, go to the "voice-lines" spreadsheet to see who's signed up for each voice line. Multiple people may sign up for a single universe, but only one will be accepted into the campaign. (see: judging).

The voice-lines are divided into three categories:

  • One-off: Most Cave variants only appear in a single chamber — great for solo mappers looking for inspiration. How does this world look and feel?

  • Series: Some universes span multiple levels, forming a "series". Each level in a given series must have continuity, making it ideal for small teams desiring something more ambitious. Is this a mini-campaign with its own story, or just a series of consecutive chambers?

  • Prime: Cave Prime voice lines don't hint at any specific universe, leaving endless artistic possibilities! Just make sure the art-style stays coherent.

Once you've chosen a voice line, fill out the "sign-up form" to claim your universe. This will automatically add you to the spreadsheet.

Now you're ready to build! There are a few technical requirements to follow, but overall, it's up to you how you want to approach this!

Once you're finished, use the "summit" form to submit the map. We will contact you if there are any complications regarding your submission.

RULES

  • Include your assigned voice line. It should ideally play at the beginning of the map as the player is leaving the elevator (if applicable). Do not play any other voice lines.

  • Maps must be original. You may remaster puzzlemaker maps with the author's permission, but you need to add enough additional substance (visuals, bts gameplay, etc) to consider it transformative.

  • Hammer maps only. This project is about breaking away from the sterile, puzzlemaker aesthetic! As stated above, you are allowed to design the puzzle in the puzzlemaker first, but it must be finalized in Hammer.

  • Must be singleplayer. The mod will be singleplayer, so maps must be singleplayer (duh). Ideally, you should play as a "generic test subject" with no specific backstory.

  • No copyrighted assets. You must have permission to use (and redistribute) any third-party assets. Be sure to declare any required attribution when submitting the map. Valve assets are allowed.

  • No public release prior to event completion. You may post it on the workshop after the mod is released, but don't ruin it by preemptively spoiling your level! Screenshots and private playtesting are fine.

TECHNICAL

This collab has slightly more in-depth technical information to cover:

  • The mod will use Portal 2: Community Edition. P2CE is backwards-compatible with vanilla Portal 2, so if you don't have access yet, you can use those tools instead.

  • Ending the map: Your map should end by triggering a logic_script entity calling [script name here] This will trigger a transition sequence where the player is pulled into the next universe (unless it's a series). The stock elevator instances already call this.

  • Pack your assets If you have assets that cannot be packed (videos etc.), include them in a zip file alongside the compiled map using the correct file structure.

  • And don't forget to build your cubemaps!
